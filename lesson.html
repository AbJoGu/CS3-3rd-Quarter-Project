After some deep thought, Sir Isaac Newton formulated the three laws of motion.

#1 Law of Intertia : an object will at rest will stay at rest and an object in motion will stay in motion unless acted upon by an external force.


#2 Law of Acceleration : the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. (F = ma)


#3 Law of Action-Reaction : for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.


These laws are fundamental to understanding how objects move and interact with each other in the physical world. They form the basis for classical mechanics and have been instrumental in shaping our understanding of everything from celestial objects to smaller-than-dust particles.

Kinemeatics is the study of motion, where the forces acting on an object don't really matter. The first and most basic equation is:


v = u + at


v = d/t

Where v is final velocity and u is initial velocity, a is acceleration and t is time, plus, d is displacement.

But we cannot understand this equation without the following terms:


Displacement : Has both magnitude and direction, shortest distance between initial and final positions.

Velocity : Has both magnitude and direction, rate of change of displacement.

Acceleration : Has both magnitude and direction, rate of change of velocity.

Time : Scalar quantity, duration of an event.

Initial Velocity : Velocity at the start of a time interval.

Final Velocity : Velocity at the end of a time interval.

Average Velocity : Total displacement divided by total time.

Average Acceleration : Total change in velocity divided by total time.

Uniform Acceleration : Constant acceleration.

Non-uniform Acceleration : Changing acceleration.
